The disease I researched is Sickle Cell Anemia. With this disease, some of your blood cells turn into sickle shapes. This causes blood clots which are harmful. Sickle Cell Anemia, otherwise known as Sickle Cell Disease or SCD is a very interesting genetic disorder you will learn about.

How is Sickle Cell Anemia Inherited?

SCD is inherited when both parents are either a carrier, or have the disease themselves. It is recessive, so both parents must at least be a carrier for the possibility for offspring to have SCD.

Gene Stuff

Sickle Cell Disease is a autosomal disease, meaning it isn't sex-linked.

Parent's Report

Alterations and Accomodations

Sickle Cell Anemia brings with it some alterations to your schedule. You don't have to go out of your way for much, but some alterations must be made no matter what. Most of the changes are with your mindset instead of schedule changes, but those can be arguably harder. I found a really good source that sums it up really well. Everything that is says can be summed up in 10 points.


  1. Accept your Disease.
  2. Take good care of yourself.
  3. Pace yourself.
  4. Remember, you can do everything you want to do.
  5. Learn to control your pain.
  6. Develop and focus on some things that bring you joy and satisfaction.
  7. Establish social, emotional, economic and medical independence.
  8. Establish an effective social support network within and outside of your family.
  9. Donâ€™t waste your pain medication.
  10. Pain is a pain, but it does not necessarily mean something terrible is happening.

Other than that, the only accomodations you might need to make to you day are:


  • Time out of your day to take medicine
  • Time out of your week/month for potential surgeries and recovery time
  • Must be ready to visit doctors at sudden notice



Emotional Impact

Your entire family may be impacted by the news that someone in your family has this disease. The family member may be in lots of pain and you may wish to help them. Sadly, there's not much you can do. You can give them pain medicine and potentially get them treatment. They also may be treated differently in school because they may not be able to participate in all the school activities. They may experience more fatigue than the other students and needing to go to the doctor more frequently, they may be absent more often as well.

Financial Requirements

Sickle Cell Anemia is sadly an expensive thing. The average patiant will need to spend $460,151 on treatments in their lifetime. Monthly averages go from $892 to $2,562. The amount typically goes up with the patients age.
